Collette

This family-owned company, operational since 1918, offers guided tours to all seven continents and serves various channels, including direct-to-consumers, groups, and agents.

Task

Redesign Collette's website to improve user experience and increase conversions.

Timeline

10 months

My Role

Competitive Research, User testing, Data synthesis, Wireframes, Prototypes

Research Process

  • Competitive Research
  • Usability Tests
  • Data Synthesis

  • To initiate the project, we commenced a comprehensive research process to gain a deeper understanding of the target audience and their requirements. We utilized a combination of competitive analysis, and usability testing to collect data. Through our research, we identified several crucial insights: Customers often experience difficulties in locating the information they require, resulting in frustration and eventual abandonment of the website. The website's design is outdated and unattractive, making it challenging to interact with the content. Customers desire a more straightforward booking process that is user-friendly and straightforward to navigate. My primary responsibility was to conduct a competitive analysis of 14 direct competitors, 10 indirect competitors, and 10 e-commerce entities, to gain insight into the competitive landscape and identify opportunities and shortcomings. This information helped inform our design decisions.

  • After presenting the competitive analysis to the team and deliberating on the key priorities, I proceeded to organize unmoderated balanced comparison tests between our website and those of our competitors, as part of the preparation for the website redesign.

  • Upon collecting the outcomes of user testing on various features to be reviewed for the redesign, I incorporated those insights into the document and created a prototype to facilitate the direct sharing of information with stakeholders.

Design Process

  • Low-fi Wireframes
  • Design System
  • Developer Handoff

  • The tight project timeline forced me to use low-fidelity wireframes from a template app for XD. Unfortunately, the creative department, which was in the process of defining their brand colors and messaging, was not thrilled with the wireframes as they felt it was impeding the creative process to be given a paint-by-numbers wireframe. The project was further delayed due to department politics and unclear decision-making power, which caused us to redo work when key personnel changed, and we had to rely on ex-employees for deliverables. One contentious issue was finding a WAGAC compliant green button that was bright and accessible to everyone. Poor alignment and documentation processes resulted in an inflated project timeline and frustrated team members.

  • While waiting for final deliverables to finalize wireframes and hand them off to developers, I attempted to create a design system to maintain consistency among components and align with branding guidelines. However, attempting to keep it updated while also managing incoming projects and ongoing research proved impossible due to the tight timeline. I did not anticipate the issues that arose and the time it took to address them.

  • Prior to my tenure at Collette, there were no developer handoff tools, and UX designers were doing front-end coding. However, due to COVID, the team had to rebuild, and I was the first on the job. This meant advocating for Zeplin to balance my time and that of others in line with the project timeline.

Delivery Process

  • Reflection
  • Prototype

  • In the end, the website required an additional seven months of work and manpower due to confusion at the beginning of the project, with key personnel leaving and a reliance on ex-employees to deliver work that prolonged the timeline. My attempt to create a design system failed, and it will require a significant amount of time and effort to reflect all the changes that the project underwent. We are currently conducting moderated and unmoderated testing before the launch and compiling the information to prioritize features in the second phase of the project, which involves several more pages and an overhaul of the booking engine. We also organized the machines into categories based on their power types to make it easier to describe and comprehend on mobile.
